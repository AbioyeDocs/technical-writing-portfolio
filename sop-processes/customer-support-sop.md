# Customer Support Ticket Management SOP
**Document Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** February 20 2026
## 1. Purpose
This document defines the standardized process for receiving, categorizing, prioritizing, and resolving customer support tickets. It ensures timely response, consistent communication, and high-quality customer satisfaction across all support channels
## 2. Scope
This SOP applies to:
- Customer support agents
- Team leads
- Relevant stakeholders involved in handling customer inquiries across email, live chat, social media, and helpdesk platforms.
 It covers all ticket submitted via: 
 - Email
 - Web forms 
 - Live chats
## 3. Definitions
- **Ticket**: A recorded customer issue or request in the support system
- **SLA (Service Level Agreement):** The expected time frame for responding and resolving tickets
- **Escalation:** Forwarding complex issues to a higher-level team
## 4. Roles and Responsibilities
- **Support Agent:** Handles ticket responses and resolutions
- **Team Lead:** Monitors performance and handles escalation
- **Technical Team:** Resolves complex or system-related issues
## 5. Tools & Systems
- Zendesk (ticket management)
- Slack (internal communication)
- Jira (technical escalation)
## 6. Ticket Priority Levels
| Priority | Description     | Response Time|
|----------|-----------------|--------------|
| High     | Critical issue  | 1 hour       |
| Medium   | Non critical bug| 4 hours     |
| Low     | General inquiry  | 24 hours   |
## 7. Procedure
### Step 1: Ticket Reception
- All incoming tickets are automatically logged in Zendesk
- Each ticket is assigned a unique ID
### Step 2: Ticket Categorization
The support agent must:
- Identify issue type (billing, technical, general)
- Assign appropriate category tag
- Select priority level based on impact
### Step 3: Initial Response
- Acknowledge ticket within SLA (service level agreement) timeframe
- Use pre-approved response templates
- Request additional information if needed
- Use clear, polite, and professional language
### Step 4: Troubleshooting & Resolution
- Review customer issue details
- Replicate issue if applicable
- Provide step-by-step solution or workaround
If unresolved;
- Escalate to technical team via jira
### Step 5: Escalation Process
Escalate when;
- Issue exceeds agent expertise
- Bug/system failure is detected
- SLA risk is identified
- Customer is dissatisfied
Actions;
- Create issue in jira
- Notify team via stack
- Attach all relevant logs and screenshots
### Step 6: Resolution & Closure
- Confirm issue is resolved with customer
- Document resolution clearly
- Mark ticket as "closed"

-------------------|--------------------

## 11. Communication Guidelines
- Be clear and concise
- Avoid technical jargon
- Personalize responses
- Maintain a friendly, professional tone
## 12. Response Templates
### General Inquiry Response
Hi **[Customer Name]**,
Thank you for reaching out. We've received your request regarding **[Issue]**. Here's what you can do:
**[Steps or explanation]**
Please let us know if you need further assistance.
Best regards,
Support Team.
### Issue Resolution Response
Hi **[Customer Name]**,
We've resolved the issue you reported.
Summary of fix:
[Explain briefly]
Kindly confirm if everything is working as expected.
Best regards,
Support Team.
### Escalation Acknowledgement
Hi **[Customer Name]**,
Thank you for your patience.
Your issue has been escalated to our technical team, and we are actively working on it. We'll update you as soon as possible.
Best regards,
Support Team.
## 13. Best Practices
- Never leave a ticket without an update
- Always confirm resolution before closing
- Keep documentation up to date
