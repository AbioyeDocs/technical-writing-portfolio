# How to upload a file to Google drive
## Introduction
Google Drive is a secure, cloud-based file storage and synchronization service developed by Google, which allows users save files ( like photos, videos, documents) on the internet instead of your phone or laptop.
## Requirements
Before you begin make sure you have;
- A Google account like Gmail. You can't use Drive without one. (See step 1 if you do not have one).
- Internet Access.
- A Device like a phone, tablet or computer.
- The file you want to upload.
## Step 1:Create a Google Account
1. Go to accounts.google.com/signup.
2. Fill in your first and last name.
3. Choose a username (this will become your Gmail address).
4. Create a strong password and confirm it.
5. Click "Next".
6. Enter your phone number  (for account security and verification).
7. Enter a recovery email if you have one (optional).
8. Fill in your date of birth and gender.
9. Click "Next", read Google's terms, and click "I agree".
You now have a Gmail account.
## Step 2: Open Google Drive
1. Go to drive.google.com.
2. Click "Go to Drive" and sign in with your new Gmail.
## Step 3: Upload a file
1. Click "New" on the left side.
2. Select "File upload".
3. Browse your computer and select the file you want to upload.
4. Click "Open".
5. Wait for it to upload; it will appear in My Drive.
From a Phone:
1. Install the Google Drive App from Play Store or App Store.
2. Open it and sign in with your new Gmail.
3. Tap the + button at the bottom.
4. Choose "Upload".
5. Find and select the file on your phone.
6. Wait for it to upload: it will show in My Drive.
## Troubleshooting
### If you can't sign in:
- Make sure your internet is working.
- Double-check you typed your email and password correctly.
- If it asks for verification, use the phone number you registered with.
### If upload does not start:
- Check your internet connection. Slow internet can prevent upload.
- Try refreshing the page (on computer) or closing and reopening the app (on phone).
 -Make sure your file is not too large. Google Drive free limit is up to 15GB shared storage.
### "Storage Full" Error:
- Delete unwanted files from Google Drive, Gmail or Google Photos.
- Or upgrade your storage plan if needed.
- Always upload smaller batches if you have multiple files to avoid errors.
